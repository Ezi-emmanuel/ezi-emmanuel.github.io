Launch into Computing

This page is my e-portfolio evidence for Launch into Computing, part of the MSc Artificial Intelligence with the University of Essex (delivered through University of Essex Online). The overview and outcomes below follow the module handbook; my own artefacts, reflection, and meeting notes follow further down.

Welcome to the Launch into Computing module.

Computing is a dynamic and rapidly evolving field that underpins modern technological advancements across various industries. This module, Launch into Computing, provides students with a comprehensive introduction to core computing disciplines, including Software Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity, Data Science, and Enterprise IT Management. By exploring the fundamental principles, methodologies, and emerging trends within these fields, students develop a strong theoretical foundation while engaging in practical applications relevant to real-world enterprise computing challenges.

Throughout this 12-week module, students examine the key concepts, tools, and strategies used in modern computing, from logic and algorithms to software development, data storage, and IT risk management.

The module emphasises critical thinking, problem-solving, and professional skills, encouraging students to evaluate ethical, legal, and social implications of computing technologies. Additionally, the role of computing in fostering equality, diversity, and inclusion (EDI) is explored, highlighting its impact on business innovation, accessibility, and digital transformation.

Module aims and learning outcomes

Below is my record of what the module sets out to achieve and what students should be able to do on completion — aligned with our module handbook.

Module aims

The module aims to provide students with:

  • An introduction to the concepts of Computing, including Software Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Data Science and Cyber Security.
  • An understanding of the basic principles, processes, and procedures of software design and implementation methodologies.
  • An understanding of how functional and non-functional requirements map onto Cyber Security processes and procedures.
  • An understanding of the relevant technologies and principles that form the basis of Artificial Intelligence implementations.
  • An appreciation for current and future challenges, limitations, and opportunities in this field.
  • The ability to present critical arguments for specific actions or outcomes to a diverse audience.
  • An understanding of how computing concepts and devices can be used to overcome barriers to equality, diversity and inclusion.
  • The opportunity to reflect on and evaluate their personal development.

On completion of this module, you will be able to:

  1. Identify and critically analyse computing challenges and processes in business systems, accounting for the current enterprise landscape.
  2. Gather and synthesise information from multiple sources (including internet resources and business publications) to aid in the systematic design and analysis of computing challenges.
  3. Critically evaluate appropriate methodologies, tools and techniques to mitigate and/or solve computing issues and their business impact.
  4. Articulate the legal, social, ethical, and professional issues faced by computing professionals.

Resources and student communication

Practical notes from the module materials on where to find help and how announcements and forums are used (University of Essex Online environment).

Computing Hub

The Computing Hub acts as an aid for your studies with the University of Essex Online. It contains resources that are regularly updated and amended — such as software, mathematics for computing, and referencing guidance. Using the Hub throughout your studies is strongly recommended.

Third-party software

Some activities and/or assignments may use third-party software. Guidance from the programme includes: accounts can often be anonymous; you do not need to use personal details in the sign-up if anonymity is acceptable; accounts can be deleted after an exercise where appropriate. Where a real email is required, your University of Essex email (issued for study) may be used — access credentials are usually those used when Library / student accounts were created.

Module announcements

Announcements appear on each module home page so tutors can post assignment guidance, reminders, and seminar recordings. Announcements typically also notify your Essex email inbox depending on notification settings — check announcements regularly so you don’t miss time-sensitive information.

Student Lounge

The Student Lounge is a space to build relationships with peers, ask questions, network, and help one another. Community guidelines apply: be courteous and respectful. You can subscribe to discussion threads if you want email updates on new posts.
